_**Question**_

If $$A$$ is a nonempty bounded subset of $$\mathbb{R}$$, and the g.l.b for
$$A$$ is equals to the l.u.b for $$A$$, what can you say about $$A$$.

[//]: # _**Intuition**_

_**Ans**_

$$A$$ only consists one element; $$x \in \mathbb{R}$$.
